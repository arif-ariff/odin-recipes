Vegan Standalone Chili

Description:

Lots of fiber, micronutrients, and BEANS. It's a filling meal that is quite healthy and not too calorific. Highly recommend adding an ounce or two of sharp cheddar cheese to make it tastier and goopier.

Ingredients:

  • 3.5 oz each of (cooked):
    • Garbanzo beans
    • Black beans
    • Kidney beans
    • Pinto beans
  • 8 oz crushed tomatoes
  • 3 oz water
  • 1 red onion
  • Bag of spinach
  • 4 oz mushrooms
  • Suggested seasonings:
    • Salt
    • Nutritional yeast
    • Chipotle seasoning
    • Turmeric
    • Cumin
    • Garlic
    • Chilli powder
    • Cloves
    • Anything else you prefer honestly

Directions:

  1. Dice onions up and throw them in a pan on low-medium heat with oil
  2. Wash mushrooms and toss those into the pan too
  3. Cover them while they cook so they become soft faster
  4. While they cook, mix and rinse all the beans in a big crockpot.
  5. Drain the bean juice (save it to drink later if you want, I don't care)
  6. Once onions and mushrooms are cooked and soft, toss them into the beans and mix
  7. Let the veggies get to know each other for a minute or two
  8. Pour in the crushed tomatoes, and fill in half the tomato container with water and toss that in too
  9. Mix it up and bring to simmer
  10. While you wait for it to simmer, toss in all the seasonings
  11. The chipotle, chili powder, nutritional yeast and salt impact the taste the most, so add those according to taste
  12. Once it tastes good, let it simmer at low-medium heat for ~10 minutes
  13. Now you have enough chili for 4-6 meals!
